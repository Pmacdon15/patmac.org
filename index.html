A little bit about my self

My name is Patrick MacDonald, and I've created this site as a stepping stone towards securing a position in the tech industry. Let me take you on a journey from my initial career endeavors to my burgeoning interest in Software Development. At the age of 22, I embarked on a driving career, a decision fueled by a string of challenging, low-paying jobs and a desire for change. With youthful exuberance and a belief that I could take on the world, I chose truck driving, imagining the thrill of navigating the vastness in large trucks—and indeed, it was exhilarating.

My career as a truck driver was a source of pride, yet it was also marred by exhaustion and financial stagnation. Despite earning a decent wage, I found myself living paycheck to paycheck, unable to save and often caught in the unexpected delays of the job—like waiting for a mechanic when a tire burst or a truck malfunctioned, or coordinating pickups for my stepdaughter due to work delays. The allure of the open road was dampened by the reality of being at the mercy of the elements, leading me to yearn for a significant change.

This desire for change sparked a period of introspection. I reminisced about my high school days, where programming captivated me, offering both a challenge and a unique form of expression. Motivated by this realization, I pursued my GED, a milestone achieved in about a month, and subsequently applied to the software development program at Bow Valley College. Although initially wait listed, I utilized the interim to enrich my understanding through online courses on Udemy, reaffirming my passion for software development. By January, I was officially a student at Bow Valley, embarking on a new chapter in my life.

Now, as I navigate through my third term, with a strategic break planned for the upcoming spring and summer, I am eagerly seeking an internship or part-time opportunity in software development. This transition marks not just a shift in my career path but a significant step towards realizing my potential in the tech industry. With two terms remaining after my break, I am committed to leveraging this time to further hone my skills and prepare for a rewarding career in software development.

This site serves as a testament to my journey and a beacon for potential opportunities that lie ahead. I look forward to connecting with like-minded individuals and organizations keen on nurturing talent and innovation in the tech landscape.
